01

The Genesis: Ripples of Water

Ripple Philosophy

Reflects how knowledge starts with a single drop (idea) and spreads across the learning community.

Form Translation

Achieved through interconnected circular blocks, central courtyards, and smooth transitions.

"The vision is to design a school environment that feels open, harmonious, and deeply connected to nature. Just as water brings clarity and calmness, the spaces of the school aim to create a peaceful atmosphere that supports focused learning and emotional well-being."

The circular geometry promotes unity, inclusiveness, and continuous movement. Each ripple represents a dedicated zone of learning (academic, recreational, creative) linked together in balance, rhythm, and the dynamic yet gentle nature of water.

Circular courtyards pull winds from the pool and distribute naturally cooled air.
02

Master Plan & Zoning

Located on Hanspura Road, the campus layout is strategically oriented relative to site wind paths and adjacent water systems. The zoning creates a separation between public vehicular parking and private student learning areas.

  • Main Access: Pedestrian entry points are kept separated from heavy vehicular parking to ensure safety.
  • Recreational Zone: Dedicated basketball, tennis, and badminton courts situated at the rear of the site.
  • Administrative Core: Positioned at the entry (LVL +10800) for control and welcoming visitors.
  • Academic & Canteen: Form a semi-circle around the central pool zone.
03

Ground Floor Layout

The ground level functions as a highly fluid social and administrative interface. Circulation paths are expansive, offering seamless access to indoor blocks and outdoor courtyards.

Admin & Entry Reception, Principal's Office, Vice Principal, and Staff Room.
Assembly & Aud. Canteen, kitchen, medical room, assembly area, and a large auditorium with back green rooms.
First Class Block Classrooms 6A, 6B, 6C are located on the ground floor with ramped accessibility (1:12).
04

Vertical Zoning & Upper Levels

As you ascend through levels (LVL +3600, +7200, +10800), the spaces transition into quieter academic and specialized research rooms, capped by integrated roof gardens.

  • First Floor: Classrooms 7A-7C, 8A-8C, computer labs, library, and teacher's room.
  • Second Floor: Classrooms 9A-9C, 10A-10C, physics, chemistry, and biology labs.
  • Third Floor: Classrooms 11A-11D, 12A-12D, music, art, and dance rooms.
  • Thermal Buffer: Accessible roof gardens insulate the blocks below while providing green breakout space.
05

Sections & Elevation Profile

The transverse sections reveal how the circular forms overlap to create varying volume heights, assisting natural light penetration and internal air circulation.

The elevation treatments exhibit a mix of local materials, showcasing the exposed brick textures and porous screens that respect Jaipur’s historical architecture.

06

Passive Cooling Strategies

Designed specifically for Jaipur's hot, dry climate, the building employs traditional thermal control methods integrated into a modern system:

Cavity Walls & XPS

Outer brick layer + air gap + inner brick with XPS insulation. Drastically cuts solar heat gain and blocks outside noise.

Light Shelves

Blocks direct glare near the window while reflecting deep, diffused daylight onto the ceiling for uniform room brightness.

Stack Ventilation

Cool air enters low through brick jali openings. As it warms, it rises and escapes through clerestory windows, drawing more cool air in.

Double Roof System

The upper roof shades the lower. Trapped air between them acts as an insulating layer, venting out the ridge.

07

Solar Simulation & Design Strategy

Analytical solar mapping dictates the building's geometry and shading layout. In Jaipur, late afternoon sun on the west and south-west faces presents the highest overheating risk.

West Façade

Deep vertical louvers aligned to the late afternoon sun angle to shade classroom windows.

South Façade

Horizontal overhangs sized for high summer noon angles + recessed window boxes.

East & South-East Windows

Operable glass windows combined with external brick screens (Jali) filter morning sun.

02 / FEATURED PROJECT

Creative Art Centre

Machkund, Dholpur • 2nd Year, 3rd Semester

Designed as an artistic intervention in Machkund, Dholpur, this project integrates with the regional topography and the surrounding historic stepwells.

The design references the stepwell's stepped geometry, creating sub-ground courtyards that act as micro-climate moderators, shielding artists from Dholpur’s intense heat.

Scale 1 : 200
Key Features Topographical integration, local stone masonry, sub-ground courtyards
